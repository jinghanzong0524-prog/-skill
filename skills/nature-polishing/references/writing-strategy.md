# Writing Strategy

Academic polishing is also making reasoning legible.

Use hourglass structure: broad -> narrow -> broad.

Every important scientific statement should have claim, evidence, and boundary.

Introduction answers what is known, what remains unresolved, what question is asked, and how the study addresses it.

Results state observed facts with conditions and quantitative support.

Discussion interprets findings and states limitations.

Conclusion restates contribution, decisive evidence, and bounded implication.

Cite the source actually read and verified.
